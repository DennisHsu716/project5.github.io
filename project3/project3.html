Common varieties of Platycerium

Platycerium ridleyi

Most of them grow at the treetops, with the nutrient leaves closely attached to and wrapping around the trunk. The environment is well-ventilated and receives ample sunlight, which makes the venation patterns of the nutrient leaves more pronounced.

Platycerium wallichii

The spore leaves exhibit a bifurcation-like wide butterfly, characterized by deep leaf veins. Overall, they spread out like a fan, creating a substantial visual impression.

Platycerium ellisii

A unique species native to Madagascar, it is the only type of staghorn fern that features wide gaps in its nutritional leaf layers.

How to take care of Platycerium

Light Ray

The deer horn fern thrives in an environment with bright, indirect light to partial sunlight and should be protected from prolonged direct sunlight. When cultivating it outdoors, be sure to acclimate it gradually.

Moisture

When watering, please thoroughly soak the moss covering the staghorn fern until water drips from it. Allow the moss to dry until its color turns white, while still feeling moist to the touch. Maintain this watering cycle. The frequency of watering may vary depending on the environment, so it is important to observe the plant carefully.

Air

Plants thrive in environments with adequate air circulation. Excessive heat can hinder water evaporation, resulting in black spots and root rot. When cultivating plants indoors, be sure to open windows or utilize a circulation fan to ensure proper airflow.
